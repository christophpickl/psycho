SCIENTIFIC PAPER
==================

* contents:
	* author, background
	* supervisors, reviewers
	* conflict of interest
	* dates (executed, published)
	* location

	* study methods; study selection; subjects
	* grading scale
	* used papers (how found)
	* literature references

	* inclusion/exclusion criteria
	* used media/diagrams support point to make
	* honesty (own insufficiencies)
	* clearly stated, specific goal/question
	* and actually being answered (on-topic)
	* drawed conclusion (findings; missings; advices)

* proper notation of references; [AUTHORs][DATE][TITLE][JOURNAL-italic][PAGES][LINK]

* IMRaD 4 part structure:
	// https://writingcenter.gmu.edu/guides/writing-an-imrad-report
	1. Introduction - case making
		* why is it important/necessary?
		* what motivates me? (problem/situation)
		* current research state
		* reveal a gap
		* hypothesis
	2. Methods - what done
		* how conducted study
		* sample, population, methods, equipment, ... (able to reproduce for others)
		* use past tense and passive voice
	3. Results - what found
		* only present/report facts, no interpretation/explanation/commentary (yet)
		* tables/diagrams numbered and labeled (captions above tables, beneath figures)
		* use past tense
	4. Discussion
		* summarize main findings + comment on them
		* connect them to other research
		* limitions of my study + suggest additional/future research
	X. Abstract
		* write at last, but put on top
		* short overview of the entire paper
		* about: report's purpose/importance, methods, main findings+implications
	* common problems with IMRaD
		* abstract lacks clear statement on findings
		* introduction lacks why research is important
		* method lacks details / is disorganized
		* results contains comments/explanations (instead neutrally report)
