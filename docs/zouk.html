Level 1

Note: I use the word "base" for the "boom" (first sound in a beat) and the word "snare" for the "chick" (2nd and 3rd sound in a beat). A beat, thus, is composed of base + snare + snare.

Class 1

Connecting Styles

  • Close: Leader hands on the back (usually shoulder blades, or more embraced) and elbows slightly pushed up and outwards. Follower rest on partner's body depending on distance (usually outside upper arms, or around neck/shoulders if closer).
  • Open: Hold each other's hands with a loose grip, applying no pressure with the thumbs.
  • Mixed: One side close, other open.

Stepping Styles

  • On-Spot: Step only on base; or even slower (at the very beginning of the dance, or when there is no beat).
  • Basic: Going back and front, either connecting style, leader starts left leg back. Feet are sliding on the ground (not stepping) and on different lines than partner ("within").
  • Lateral: Leader is just doing side-steps, the follower crosses her steps while always facing to the leader.

Transition from basic to lateral, by "sending follower out" (leader stays on the spot during basic), and both are changing their stepping styles.

Star

Do a 90-degree turn left (or right, which is more unnatural) and do it while the leader is in the back (or front).
Do the turn only on the snares: straight - turn - turn.

Basic step and (arbitrary) turns

Square

Do a 90-degree turn left (or right) after every beat, ending up in the same direction again after 4 turns.

From close basic, doing a square to the left, four times

Embrace

From lateral step, keep holding both hands; once follower wants to walk left, keep her in place and rotate on the spot. Identical footwork, arm is once over shoulder then rib cage height.

More has been done here in the bootcamp: simple exit from lateral to basic with holding the left hand; embrace to jojo; ...

Class 2

Slow Spot Rotation

In basic, when leader is in front, "ground" partner, and slowly rotate on spot (pivot point in between both), do small steps, and keep the rhythm.

Clear the Line

The leader steps out of the line while going backwards (crossing feet), let go of left hand and both facing the same direction. Give impulse to follower to rotate back again and step into "her line".
