Level 1

Note: I use the word "base" for the "boom" (first sound in a beat) and the word "snare" for the "chick" (2nd and 3rd sound in a beat). A beat, thus, is composed of base + snare + snare.

Class 1

Connecting Styles

  • Close: Leader hands on the back (usually shoulder blades, or more embraced), follower rest on partner's body depending on distance (usually outside upper arms, or around neck/shoulders).
  • Open: Hold hands with loose grip, no thumbs.
  • Mixed: Either side different.

Stepping Styles

  • Staying: Step only on base; or even slower (at the beginning, or when there is no beat).
  • Lateral: Leader just side steps, follower facing to leader and crosses steps.
  • Basic: Going back and front, either connecting style, leader starts left leg back. Feet are sliding on the ground (less stepping) and on different lines than partner ("within").

Transition from basic to lateral, by "sending follower out" (leader stays on spot during basic), and changing stepping styles for both.

Star

Do a 90-degrees turn left (or right) and do it while leader is in the back (or front).
Do the turn only on the snares: straight - turn - turn.

Square

Do a 90-degrees turn left (or right) after every beat, ending up same direction again after 4 turns.

Embrace

From lateral step, keep holding both hands; once follower wants to walk left, keep her at place and rotate on spot. Identical footwork, arm is once over shoulder then ribcage height.

More has been done here in the bootcamp: simple exit from lateral to basic with left hand holding; embrace to jojo; ...

Class 2

Slow Spot Rotation

In basic, when leader is in front, "ground" partner, and slowly rotate on spot (pivot point in between both), do small steps, and keep the rhythm.

Clear the Line

Leader steps out of the line while going backwards (cross feet), let go of left hand and both facing same direction. Give impulse to follower to rotate back again and step into "her line".
