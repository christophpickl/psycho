Basics

  • The Arms: Leader around the waist, follower on the closer shoulder blade (or somewhere on the back, or even arm if height difference), not on top of the shoulder!
  • The Hands: Leader palm facing up, follower resting inside, 4 fingers have a sponge-spring quality (connecting the frame; enough tension so no need to squeeze; shape locks together), thumbs are free. The arms holding the hands are totally relaxed, hanging like noodles.
  • The Rhythms: 6-count, 8-count, Charleston, ...
  • Quality: Considering it's an African diasporic dance, it's more lively (less upright ballroom European stiff) and danced to big band jazz with a lot of bouncy energy.
  • Footwork: Staying in place, traveling (default), moving in/out (rock step with lateral).
  • Positions: Closed position (side-by-side, closer together), and open position (in front of each other, further away; basic-step can be same or opposite direction).

Steps

We usually step quick (one beat) or slow (two beats), which can also be a triple step. The quick-steps come in pairs, also called rock-steps.

Keep a bounce and bend the (soft) knees; for the slow-step (without triples) we do 2 bounces (connect to the music).
Slightly lean forward with your upper body, and also keep most of the weight on the front part of the foot.
Turn your direction while doing the basic step.

8-Count Step

Stepping pattern is: Quick (1) - Quick (2) - Slow (3+4), Quick (5) - Quick (6) - Slow (7+8).

6-Count Step

Stepping pattern is: Quick (1) - Quick (2) - Slow (3+4) - Slow (5+6).

Repeater

Simply repeating the same stepping parts on the (semi) spot. For this, it is crucial to have a good, strong connection with your partner: Follower's elbow pushed inwards, leader's embrace harm tight; don't be shy to give a clear push/pull.
Different possibilities are available:

  • While being at the back/rock-step (open position), instead of a slow-step, stay on the spot and hover the leg in front.
  • While being at the front/5-6 (open position), repeat the quick-quick-steps. The outer leg stays on the spot still is lifted a bit when the weight is shifted off; and the inner leg constantly moves back and forth (no slow-step). To stop the repeater, make the back-step quick-step into a slow-step. Also, while the repeater-quick-step keeps the more to the front, and the leader is being given a slight impulse forward ("stop"), when stopping, the whole body is moved backwards, and an impulse to move backwards is given ("go").
  • While being in the closed position, we do a similar step pattern as with being in the front open position. Be clear as a leader to "zap" at the edges with your arms, and to rotate the partner back in to go back to the open position. Here either leader or follower in front will change how steps and who stays.
  • There are also oppositions where we go counter back-and-forth, yet with the same quality of repeaters.

Break

When the music is not playing (it has a break), we can stand still on the spot. We do so at the back-most position (stay on back-leg, the 2nd quick-step), and indicate by being upright with the upper body (instead of leaning forward).

Positioning

Simple techniques to change your positions.

Pass-By

Also called "change positions" (nomen est omen), where we start in the open position and do an async basic-step (opposite direction). Leader raises arm straight up, doing an internal rotation (as if would look at the watch) on the 2nd count/quick, and rotating the follower CCW.
Remember to do small steps (otherwise end up too far away from each other), and to not cross your steps.

Butterfly

To indicate first externally rotate a bit, and then both rotate 90 degrees (half-turn) towards the partner, opening "their wings". Try to start with left and right leg, as well as clockwise and counter-clockwise.

Here is a nice possibility to change the roles; just watch out to get the hand position right by putting the leader's arm underneath first.

Send-out

A send-out is basically moving from closed- to open-position, with either leader or follower in front.

  • To send out the follower, the leader stays on the spot and slightly pulls with the left hand. Rotate your torso towards the follower, and push with your right hand to rotate the follower and make her step in front of you.
  • The leader send-out is done by first indicating to the follower to stay on the spot (otherwise tricky traveling far), and then move in front; continue with the basic-step or move back again to a side-by-side position.
